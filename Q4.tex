\section{Question 4}
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\alph*)]
    \item % Part(a) 
    By HB C3 3.8 p76, the inverse of $\infty$ with respect to a circle is its centre. \\
    So $\beta = 1$
    \vvs \\
    \item  % Part (b)
    From the Theorem HB C3 3.6 p75,
    \begin{align*}
        f(\alpha) &= \frac{ \infty - 2i }{ i\infty - 1 } \to \frac{1}{i} = -i \\
        f(\beta)  &= \frac{ 1-2i }{ i-1 } = \frac{ (1-2i)(-1-i)}{2} \\
        &= \frac{1}{2} ( -3+i ) = -\frac{3}{2} + \frac{i}{2}.
    \end{align*}
    Then,
    \[ |z - (-i)| = k\left| z - \left(-\frac{3}{2} + \frac{i}{2}\right) \right|. \]
    \vs \\
    Now the point 0 is on $C$, so $f(0)$ is on $f(C)$:
    \[ f(0) = \frac{-2i}{-1} = 2i. \]
    \vs \\
    Thus,
    \[ \left| 2i - (-i) \right| = k \left| 2i - \left(-\frac{3}{2} + \frac{i}{2}\right) \right| = k \left| \frac{3}{2} + \frac{3}{2}i\right| = k \cdot \frac{3}{2}\left| 1 + i\right| = k \cdot \frac{3 \sqrt{2}}{2}; \]
    \vs \\
    so
    \[ k = \frac{|3i|}{(3\sqrt{2})/2} = \frac{2}{\sqrt{2}} = \sqrt{2}.\]
    \vs \\
    Hence, the equation for the image circle $f(C)$ in Apollonian form is
    \[ \left|z + i\right| = \sqrt{2}\left| z + \frac{3}{2} - \frac{i}{2}\right|.\]
    \vvs \\
    \item % Part (c)
    By HB C3 3.11 p76, with $\alpha = -i$, $\beta = (-3 + i)/2$, and $k = \sqrt{2} \neq 1$, the centre of the circle is
    \vs \\
    \[ \lambda = \frac{\alpha - k^2 \beta}{1 - k^2} = \frac{ -i - 2\left( -3 + i \right)/2 }{ 1-2 } = \frac{-i + 3 - i}{-1} = -3 + 2i; \]
    \vs \\
    and the radius is 
    \[ r = \frac{ k \left| \alpha - \beta \right| }{\left| 1 - k^2 \right|} = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{|-1|} \cdot \left|  - i -\left( \frac{-3 + i}{2}\right)\right| = \sqrt{2} \left| \frac{3}{2} - \frac{3}{2}i\right| = \frac{3\sqrt{2}}{2} \left| 1 - i \right| = 3.\]
\end{enumerate}
