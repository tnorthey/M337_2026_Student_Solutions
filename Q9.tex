        \item 
        \begin{align*}
    \text{Let }
    f(z) &= (z+i)^3 e^{-z} - \alpha \\
    g(z) &= (z+i)^3 e^{-z}. \\
\end{align*}
Let $ \mathcal{R} $ be the simply connected region $\C$ and $ \Gamma \text{ be} \{z: |z| = 2 \} $ 
\vs \\
Then $f$ and $g$ are analytic on $ \mathcal{R} $, and $ \Gamma $ is a simple-closed contour in $ \mathcal{R} $.
\vs \\
So, for $ z \in \Gamma $
\begin{align*}
    | f(z) - g(z) | &= |-\alpha| \\
    &< 1, \qquad \text{by the Triangle Inequality} \\
    &\leq |g(z)|
\end{align*}
Thus, by Rouché's Theorem, $f$ has the same number of zeros as $g$ inside $\Gamma$, namely $3$, since $-i$ ia zero of order 3 and $ e^{-z} \neq 0 $ on $\C$. 
\vs \\
Hence, $ (z+i)^3 e^{-z} = \alpha $ has 3 solutions on the disc $ \{z: |z| < 2 \} $.
